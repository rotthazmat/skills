# Humanization Techniques

Reference for Step 2 and Step 3. Each technique includes a before/after in English and Spanish.

---

## 1. Vary Sentence Length Deliberately

Human writing has rhythm: short punchy sentences land next to long winding ones. AI tends to produce uniform mid-length sentences.

**Pattern to break:** every sentence is 15-25 words.

**English before:**
> The project presented numerous challenges that required careful consideration and thoughtful planning from the entire team.

**English after:**
> The project was a mess. We burned two weeks on something that should have taken three days, mostly because nobody agreed on what "done" meant.

**Spanish before:**
> El proyecto presentó numerosos desafíos que requirieron una cuidadosa consideración y una planificación reflexiva por parte de todo el equipo.

**Spanish after:**
> El proyecto fue un caos. Quemamos dos semanas en algo que debería haber tomado tres días, principalmente porque nadie se puso de acuerdo en qué significaba "terminado".

---

## 2. Use Contractions and Spoken Register

Formal writing can still sound human. But when contractions are appropriate, use them. Avoiding them entirely is an AI tell.

**English:** *don't, it's, I've, we're, they'd, wouldn't* instead of *do not, it is, I have, we are, they would, would not*

**Spanish:** Spanish contracts less (only *al* and *del*), so humanization works differently:
- Use personal verb forms instead of impersonal constructions
- Use short colloquial forms: *qué va* instead of *de ninguna manera*, *claro* instead of *por supuesto*
- Regional expressions: *oye*, *mira*, *fíjate que*, *resulta que* (depending on register)

---

## 3. Replace Generic Examples with Specific Ones

AI examples are broad enough to fit anyone — which means they fit no one. Specific detail is the fastest way to read as human.

**English before:**
> For example, a busy professional might find it difficult to maintain a healthy work-life balance.

**English after:**
> My coworker Marcus hasn't taken a vacation day in three years. He brings his laptop to birthday parties. Nobody calls this a problem.

**Spanish before:**
> Por ejemplo, un profesional ocupado podría encontrar difícil mantener un equilibrio entre el trabajo y la vida personal.

**Spanish after:**
> Mi amiga Lucía no ha cogido vacaciones en tres años. Lleva el portátil a los cumpleaños. Y nadie lo ve como un problema.

---

## 4. Commit to Opinions — Remove Hedge Clusters

One hedge is fine. Three in one paragraph read as machine output. Identify the core claim and state it.

**English before:**
> This approach may potentially offer some benefits in certain situations, though results could vary depending on individual circumstances.

**English after:**
> This works. Not for everyone — but if you're dealing with a distributed team, it's the right call.

**Spanish before:**
> Este enfoque podría ofrecer potencialmente algunos beneficios en determinadas situaciones, aunque los resultados pueden variar según las circunstancias individuales.

**Spanish after:**
> Esto funciona. No para todo el mundo, pero si tienes un equipo distribuido, es lo que toca hacer.

---

## 5. Show Emotions, Don't Describe Them

AI reports emotional states from a detached observer position. Human writing puts you inside the feeling.

**English before:**
> The experience was quite difficult and emotionally taxing for those involved.

**English after:**
> I didn't sleep for two nights. Not because of the workload — I just kept replaying the conversation.

**Spanish before:**
> La experiencia resultó bastante difícil y emocionalmente agotadora para las personas involucradas.

**Spanish after:**
> No dormí en dos noches. No era la carga de trabajo. Era que seguía repasando la conversación en bucle.

---

## 6. Cut Formulaic Openers and Closers

The opening sentence is the most AI-flagged part of a text. The closing is the second most. Rewrite both from scratch.

### Openers: cut or replace

**English — cut:**
- ~~"In today's fast-paced world, it is more important than ever to..."~~
- Replace with: the specific thing you're actually saying. Start mid-thought if needed.

**Spanish — cut:**
- ~~"En el mundo actual, vivimos en una era de constantes cambios donde..."~~
- Replace with: the observation, argument, or scene that motivated the piece.

### Closers: avoid the summary recap

**English — avoid:**
- ~~"In conclusion, we have seen that X, Y, and Z are all important considerations."~~
- Replace with: an implication, an open question, or a single forward-looking sentence.

**Spanish — avoid:**
- ~~"En conclusión, hemos podido observar que X, Y y Z son aspectos fundamentales."~~
- Replace with: una consecuencia, una pregunta abierta, o una frase que cierre con peso propio.

---

## 7. Break Structural Scaffolding

Remove the visible skeleton of the text. A reader should feel the argument, not see its outline.

**Technique:** After rewriting, delete any sentence that is purely structural — it only exists to announce what comes next.

**English structural sentence (cut):**
- ~~"In the following sections, I will explore three key aspects of this issue."~~
- ~~"Having established the background, we can now turn to the main argument."~~

**Spanish structural sentence (cut):**
- ~~"A continuación, analizaré los tres aspectos principales de esta cuestión."~~
- ~~"Una vez establecido el contexto, procedemos a examinar el argumento central."~~

---

## 8. Use Punctuation Naturally

**Em dashes (English):** Use sparingly — once per page at most. When you catch yourself using one, ask if a comma or period would work.

**Ellipses:** Human writers use them to trail off, create pause, or imply something unsaid. AI rarely uses them; adding one or two in appropriate moments can help.

**Fragments:** A one-word sentence is fine. "Exactly." "No." "Never." AI avoids fragments because they feel grammatically incomplete; humans use them for rhythm.

**Spanish punctuation notes:**
- Exclamation and question marks open and close: *¿así que no sabías?*, *¡qué barbaridad!* — use them where natural, not performatively
- Parentheses for asides are more natural in Spanish prose than em dashes

---

## 9. Introduce One Structural Irregularity

A single unexpected move signals a human wrote this:

- A paragraph that starts with a number: *"Three years ago, I would have disagreed."*
- A direct address to the reader mid-essay: *"Think about the last time you had to explain this to someone."*
- A very short paragraph (one sentence) after a long one
- A sentence that starts with *And* or *But* (grammatically acceptable; AI avoids it)
- In Spanish: starting a sentence with *Y* or *Pero*, using a dash to introduce a qualification, or an abrupt shift in register

---

## 10. Language-Specific Humanization

### English
- Use contractions consistently
- Prefer Anglo-Saxon words over Latinate ones (*use* not *utilize*, *start* not *commence*, *show* not *demonstrate*)
- One or two informal markers where register allows (*Look,*, *The thing is,*, *Honestly,*)
- Read the rewrite aloud — if a sentence is hard to say, it sounds like writing, not speech

### Spanish
- Use first-person active verbs: *hice* instead of *se realizó*, *creo* instead of *cabe creer*
- Avoid impersonal constructions: *se puede ver que* → *vemos que*, *es importante destacar* → *hay que decirlo*
- Use regional markers naturally: *oye*, *mira*, *es que*, *resulta que*, *fíjate que* (calibrate to Spanish variant: peninsular, mexicano, rioplatense, etc.)
- Vary clause order — Spanish has more freedom than English; move elements around to avoid the subject-verb-object lockstep
- Short sentences in Spanish are more marked (less common) than in English — use them for emphasis, not as a default

---

## 11. Flatten "is/are" Avoidance

AI replaces simple verbs with elaborate substitutes (*serves as, stands as, marks, represents, boasts*) to seem sophisticated. Reverse this.

**English before:**
> This framework *serves as* a cornerstone of modern development and *represents* a major shift in how teams operate.

**English after:**
> This framework is the cornerstone of modern development. It changed how teams work.

**Spanish before:**
> Este enfoque *constituye* un pilar fundamental y *representa* un cambio significativo en la forma en que los equipos operan.

**Spanish after:**
> Este enfoque es la base del trabajo moderno. Cambió cómo trabajan los equipos.

---

## 12. Break Negative Parallelisms

The "Not X, it's Y" construction is a statistical signature of AI writing. Rewrite as a direct claim.

**English before:**
> It's not about working harder, it's about working smarter. It's not just a tool, it's a mindset shift.

**English after:**
> Working smarter beats working harder. And it starts with the tools you choose.

**Spanish before:**
> No se trata de trabajar más, sino de trabajar mejor. No es solo una herramienta, es un cambio de mentalidad.

**Spanish after:**
> Trabajar mejor importa más que trabajar más. Y eso empieza por las herramientas que eliges.

---

## 13. Trim Appended "-ing" Clauses

Participial phrases tacked onto sentence endings inflate length without adding meaning. Cut them or promote them to standalone sentences if they matter.

**English before:**
> The team delivered the report on time, *demonstrating their commitment to quality and showcasing their collaborative spirit*.

**English after:**
> The team delivered on time.

*(If the quality detail actually matters:)*
> The team delivered on time. It was the third tight deadline in a row.

**Spanish before:**
> El equipo entregó el informe a tiempo, *demostrando su compromiso con la calidad y evidenciando su espíritu colaborativo*.

**Spanish after:**
> El equipo entregó a tiempo. Era el tercer plazo ajustado seguido.

---

## 14. Replace Vague Authority Attributions

"Experts say" and "studies show" without specifics are AI filler. Either name the source, drop the attribution, or reframe as personal experience.

**English before:**
> Studies show that remote work increases productivity. Experts argue that flexibility is key to employee satisfaction.

**English after:**
> A 2023 Stanford study tracked 16,000 workers over nine months and found a 13% productivity jump. The main driver wasn't flexibility — it was the commute they no longer had.

*(If no source exists, own the claim:)*
> From what I've seen, remote workers get more done. Not because of the setup — because of the commute they cut.

**Spanish before:**
> Los estudios demuestran que el teletrabajo aumenta la productividad. Los expertos señalan que la flexibilidad es clave.

**Spanish after:**
> Un estudio de Stanford siguió a 16.000 trabajadores durante nueve meses y encontró un aumento del 13% en productividad. El factor principal no era la flexibilidad: era el trayecto que dejaban de hacer.

---

## 15. Deflate Wordcount Inflation

AI adds length to seem thorough. Tighten by cutting sentences that restate what the previous sentence already said.

**Test:** Can this sentence be removed without losing meaning? If yes, remove it.

**English before:**
> This is a critical issue that demands our immediate attention. The urgency of addressing this matter cannot be overstated. It is imperative that we act now.

**English after:**
> Act now.

**Spanish before:**
> Esta es una cuestión crítica que requiere nuestra atención inmediata. La urgencia de abordar este asunto no puede subestimarse. Es imperativo que actuemos cuanto antes.

**Spanish after:**
> Hay que actuar ya.

---

## 16. Argue, Don't Summarize

AI defaults to describing and praising rather than claiming and supporting. In any analytical or argumentative text, ensure the writing *does the thing* rather than *talking about the thing*.

**Diagnosis questions:**
- Does this paragraph make a claim, or describe that a claim exists?
- Is there specific evidence, or just a high-vocabulary assertion?
- Would a reader finish this knowing *your* position, or just *the topic*?

**English before:**
> This novel effectively explores themes of identity and belonging through its complex characters, offering readers a thought-provoking meditation on what it means to find one's place in the world.

**English after:**
> The novel argues that belonging is always borrowed. Every character who finds community immediately starts policing who else can enter it — which makes the ending, where the protagonist finally "fits in," read as a loss, not a triumph.

**Spanish before:**
> Esta novela explora de manera efectiva los temas de identidad y pertenencia a través de sus complejos personajes, ofreciendo al lector una reflexión muy profunda sobre lo que significa encontrar el propio lugar en el mundo.

**Spanish after:**
> La novela sostiene que la pertenencia siempre es prestada. Cada personaje que encuentra comunidad empieza enseguida a decidir quién más puede entrar. Eso hace que el final, cuando la protagonista por fin "encaja", se lea como una pérdida, no como un logro.

---

## 17. Preserve Technical Terms — Never Paraphrase Them

AI's synonym-variation habit bleeds into proper nouns, technical terms, and established titles. This is one of the most jarring tells in academic and professional writing. The fix is simple: use the correct term, always.

**Rule:** If a term has an official name — a legal term, a scientific concept, a proper title, a named framework — use that name. Do not vary it.

**English before:**
> The defendant was transferred from the court system for minors to the adult legal proceedings structure.

**English after:**
> The defendant was transferred from juvenile court to adult court.

**Spanish before:**
> El acusado fue trasladado del sistema judicial para menores al procedimiento legal de adultos.

**Spanish after:**
> El acusado fue trasladado del tribunal de menores al tribunal ordinario.

**Test:** If a subject-matter expert would wince at the phrasing, it's wrong.

## 18. End the Conclusion Once

AI pads conclusions to meet word counts, often producing two or three closing paragraphs that say the same thing in different words. A conclusion should end once, sharply.

**Pattern to cut:** Any closing paragraph that starts with "In summary," "To conclude," "As we have seen," or "En definitiva," after you have already written one closing paragraph.

**English before:**
> In conclusion, this analysis has shown that renewable energy is essential for addressing climate change. To summarize, the evidence reviewed here demonstrates the critical importance of transitioning away from fossil fuels. Ultimately, it is clear that bold action is required.

**English after:**
> Renewable energy isn't an option anymore. It's the only path that doesn't compound the problem.

**Spanish before:**
> En conclusión, este análisis ha demostrado que las energías renovables son esenciales. En resumen, las evidencias revisadas muestran la importancia crítica de la transición. En definitiva, queda claro que se requiere una acción decidida.

**Spanish after:**
> Las energías renovables no son una opción más. Son el único camino que no agrava el problema.

## 19. Stay Accountable to Your Sources

Human writers are bound by their sources. Every conclusion should be traceable back to evidence actually presented. This is one of the clearest consistency signals that separates human from AI writing.

### Three rules
1. **Don't claim more than your sources support.** If the evidence is narrow, the conclusion is narrow.
2. **Don't contradict yourself.** If you define, attribute, or assert something early in the text, be consistent with it throughout.
3. **Don't cite a source for a claim it doesn't make.** Either find the real source or own the claim as your own inference.

### Diagnosing an overreaching conclusion

Ask: *Does the evidence in the body of this text actually lead to this conclusion?*

**English before:**
> A 2022 study of 200 university students found that short sleep duration correlated with lower exam scores. This demonstrates that sleep deprivation is the primary cause of academic failure across all educational levels.

**English after:**
> A 2022 study of 200 university students found that short sleep duration correlated with lower exam scores. It's a narrow sample, but it points in the same direction as larger sleep research — less sleep, worse performance.

**Spanish before:**
> Un estudio de 2022 con 200 estudiantes universitarios encontró correlación entre poco sueño y peores calificaciones. Esto demuestra que la falta de sueño es la causa principal del fracaso escolar en todos los niveles educativos.

**Spanish after:**
> Un estudio de 2022 con 200 estudiantes universitarios encontró correlación entre poco sueño y peores calificaciones. Es una muestra pequeña, pero apunta en la misma dirección que investigaciones más amplias: menos sueño, peor rendimiento.

### Diagnosing internal contradictions

Read the text from conclusion back to opening. Check:
- Does the conclusion claim something never established in the body?
- Is any source attributed two different positions?
- Is any term defined differently in different sections?

If yes — fix the claim, not the evidence. Don't add new evidence to shore up an overreaching conclusion; scale the conclusion down to match what you have.
